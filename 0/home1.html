Welcome to the Cupid Research Study

Cannabinol Use for Patients with Insomnia Disorder (CUPID)

We invite you to participate in an exciting new research study that involves testing a cannabis-based medication, containing cannabinol (CBN), that may help improve sleep and daytime function in people with chronic insomnia.

You may be eligible to participate in this study if you:

Have chronic insomnia (persistent problems falling or staying asleep)

Are aged 25 to 60 years

Live in Sydney or Greater Sydney

This study will test the effects of two doses of a cannabis-based medicine on sleep and daytime function in people with chronic insomnia.
If eligible, you will need to come into the Woolcock Institute of Medical Research in Glebe, Sydney and stay in one of our overnight sleep suites on four occasions.
We will measure various aspects of your sleep and cognition over an 18-hour period.
You will receive reimbursement for your time and, at no expense to you, study medication and study-related evaluations from a doctor.

Your participation in this study is voluntary.
